Social Pressure to be Busy and Emotional Wellbeing

A seven-day daily diary study examining the relationship between daily perceived social pressure to be busy and emotional wellbeing across 261 participants. Multilevel modelling revealed that daily social pressure to be busy predicted daily negative affect, anxiety, and depressive symptoms at the within-person level. Trait emotional complexity acceptance moderated these relationships, with those lower on acceptance reporting significantly worse wellbeing on days they felt greater social pressure to be busy. Ideal for teaching multilevel modelling, within-person centering, and moderation in a longitudinal context.

Suggested Learning Objectives

  • Explain the difference between between-person and within-person effects
  • Apply group mean centering to separate within- and between-person variance
  • Fit a multilevel model with person-level and day-level predictors
  • Interpret fixed and random effects in a longitudinal model

Suggested Classroom Activities

  • Compare a naive OLS regression ignoring clustering to a multilevel model, and discuss what changes
  • Visualize within-person trajectories of busyness and affect across days

Common Student Misconceptions

  • Treating repeated observations from the same person as independent
  • Confusing within-person and between-person effects of busyness on wellbeing
